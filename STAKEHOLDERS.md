# Stakeholder Analysis – Student Grade Management System

| Stakeholder              | Role                                                  | Key Concerns                                                         | Pain Points                                                 | Success Metrics                                           |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Lecturer                 | Captures and manages student grades for subjects.     | Accurate grade recording, easy grade entry, quick access to results. | Manual spreadsheets are time-consuming and prone to errors. | Grades recorded quickly and accurately for all students.  |
| Students                 | Receive grades and monitor academic performance.      | Access to accurate and timely grade information.                     | Delays in receiving academic results.                       | Students can view grades quickly after they are captured. |
| University Administrator | Oversees academic records and reporting.              | Reliable academic data for decision making and reporting.            | Difficulty compiling results from multiple sources.         | Academic reports generated faster and accurately.         |
| IT Support Staff         | Maintains and supports the system infrastructure.     | System stability, maintainability, and security.                     | Systems that crash frequently or are difficult to maintain. | System uptime of 99% and minimal maintenance issues.      |
| Registrar                | Responsible for maintaining official student records. | Accurate student records and grade storage.                          | Manual record management increases risk of data loss.       | All academic records stored securely and retrievable.     |
| System Developer         | Designs and maintains the system.                     | Clear requirements and scalable system architecture.                 | Poor documentation or unclear requirements.                 | System can be easily updated and maintained.              |
