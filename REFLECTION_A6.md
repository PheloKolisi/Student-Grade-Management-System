# Reflection – Assignment 6

One of the main challenges in this assignment was translating system requirements into user stories while ensuring they followed Agile principles such as being small, testable, and valuable. It required breaking down technical requirements into user-focused needs.

Another challenge was prioritizing the backlog using the MoSCoW method. It was sometimes difficult to decide which features were essential and which could be delayed, especially when all features seemed important for system functionality.

Effort estimation was also challenging because it required predicting how long tasks would take without actual development experience for all features. Assigning story points required careful judgment and understanding of task complexity.

Additionally, planning the sprint required balancing workload and ensuring that selected tasks could realistically be completed within the two-week sprint period.

Overall, this assignment helped in understanding how Agile methodologies support structured and flexible system development while ensuring continuous delivery of value.
